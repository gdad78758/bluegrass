Moroccan Chicken Stew


Prep:

For the Chicken
  1. Get the spice rub ready, combine in small bowl:
        1 teaspoon smoked paprika
        1 teaspoon cumin
        1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
        1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
        1/8 teaspoon ground cloves
		
	 Mix well
		
  2. Prepare 
        1/2 onion, chopped
        3 cloves of garlic, minced
        1 medium zucchini, cubed
        1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
        6 ounces jarred roasted peppers, sliced
        1/2 cup green olives, pitted and halved
        1 teaspoon parsley, chopped
        4 thin slices of lemon
        1/2 red chili, thinly sliced, or pinch of red pepper flakes
        1 1/2 cup chicken broth
        avocado or olive oil
        salt
        pepper
		
  3. Season the chicken with a generous pinch of salt and half the spice rub on one side, flip and repeat.
     
  4. Preheat a wide pan ust under high heat for 2 minutes.
  
  5. Add 1 tablespoon of oil, wait 30 seconds, and add the chicken to the pan. 
     If your pan is not big enough, do this in 2 batches.
	 
  6. Cook chicken for 2-3 minutes until the crust is brown and crusty.
  
  7. Flip the chicken and repeat cooking for 2-3 minutes.
  
  8. Remove chicken from pan.
  
  9. Lower heat to medium, add to the pan:
        2 teaspoons oil to the pan 
        onions
        zucchini 
        thyme
        1/4 teaspoon salt 
        few cracks of pepper.
	Cook for 10 minutes, stirring often.
	 
  10. Add garlic and cook for 3 minutes.
  
  11. Add:
        the roasted peppers
        olives
        chicken, along with any juices
        add enough chicken stock to come halfway up the side of the chicken.
    
  12. Tuck lemon slices around the chicken and add the sliced chiles
  
  13. Bring the liquid to a boil, reduce to a simmer, cook uncovered for 20-25 minutes,
      or until the stock has reduced considerably and can be used as a sauce. Move the
	  chicken around the pan a couple of times so everything cooks evenly.
	  
  14. Sprinkle parsley of the dish when the chicken is ready and set aside.
		
		
For Cauliflower rice:

  1. Prepare
        1 large head of cauliflour
        1/2 onion, chopped
        3 cloves garlic, minced
        1 teaspoon fresh grated ginger
        1 tablespoon ground turmeric powder
        1 cup full-fat coconut milk
        3 tablespoons unsweeted shredded coconut flakes
        1/4 cup blanched almonds or walnuts, chopped and roasted if desired
        zest of 1/2 lime
        zest of 1/2 orange
        1 tablespoon parsley, chopped
        1/2 red chili, thinly sliced
        avocado oil
        salt
        pepper
	  
  2. Cut head of cauliflour in quarters and grate on largest setting of box grater,
     trying not to get too much of the stalk.
	
  3. Preheat large pan with high sides over medium heat for 2 minutes.
  
  4. Add:
        1 tablespoon oil
        onions
        1/4 teaspoon salt
        couple cracks of pepper
	  
  5. Cook for 6 minutes
  
  6. Add garlic and cook for 2 minutes
  
  7. Add tumeric powder and mix well.  Cook for 45 seconds.
  
  8. Add coconut milk and mix well.  Bring to a simmer and cook for 3 minutes, until 
     it has reduced some.
	 
  9. Add
        cauliflour rice
        1/4 teaspoon salt
        few cracks of pepper
	 Mix very well.
	 
  10. Place a lid, or sheet tray, on the pan, and cook for 3 minutes.
  
  11. Check to see if the cauliflour is done to your liking and, if not, cook
      another 2 minutes.
	  
  12. Turn the heat off, then add:
        coconut flakes
        nuts
        lime and orange zest
        parsley
        red chilies
	   
  13. Mix well and check for seasoning.  You can add lime juice or salt if desired.
  
  
  Serve!
